The sky's color changes based off of multiple factors.

They are as follows, in a simple form.

  • The time of day
  • Whatever is in the air at the time
  • The spread of particals/ molicules within the autosphere

But why is that?

From what i know, it is something called scattering. Scattering is where the molicules within the air scatter the waves of light, effecting how our eyes see the waves as a color. Sometimes they alter the waves of light or straight up block some making it so only longer waves, such as red or orange, make it through.
